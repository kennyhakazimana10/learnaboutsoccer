Soccer Positions & Strategies

Learn how players work together on the field

Soccer, or football, is played between two teams of eleven players. It’s a game of skill, strategy, and teamwork. The game consists of two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute halftime break. Key rules include the offside rule, where attacking players cannot be ahead of the second-to-last defender when the ball is passed, and fouls, which result in free kicks or penalty kicks for serious offenses.

Soccer has four main positional groups: goalkeepers, defenders, midfielders, and forwards (or attackers). Each group contains specialized roles like center-backs and wingers, and team formations dictate specific responsibilities.

Essential Gear

  • Soccer ball
  • Shin guards
  • Cleats
  • Team jersey and shorts

Basic Rules

  1. No hands allowed (except goalkeeper)
  2. Game divided into two 45-minute halves
  3. Offside rule must be followed
  4. Fouls are penalized with free kicks or penalties
